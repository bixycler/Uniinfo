The FoC (Form ∘ Content)

The Trinion (\(\Trinion\)) is not an inanimate object, but an alive being with its dynamic (↑) and balance (=). However, looking back at itself, the Trinion cannot see all of its components at once, but only some abstraction of itself.

As the eye of a subject, the Equal (=) is first abstracted so that the Trinion sees its body as an object composed of the Circle (◯) and the Arrow (↑). Abstracting the dynamic (↑), the Trinion takes "snapshot" of its form as a static Circle (◯). After many snapshots, it connects them into a sequence then further abstracts the form to infer its content as a static Arrow (↑). Finally, it reasons about the relation between what it has seen to draw the conclusion «◯ = ↑», then abstracts the two known symbols Circle (◯) and Arrow (↑) to infer that its operator is the Equal (=).

  • Postulate of Existence: There exists a Circle (◯).
  • Postulate of Differentiation: There exists an Arrow (↑) different from the Circle.
  • Postulate of Unification: The Circle and the Arrow are two aspects of a united body via the Equal (=)[4].
